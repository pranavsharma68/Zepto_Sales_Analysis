# Zepto Sales Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Zepto sales and inventory data to uncover insights related to customer purchasing behavior, product performance, pricing strategies, and sales trends. The objective was to transform raw quick-commerce transactional data into actionable business insights using SQL and Power BI.

The project focuses on identifying revenue-driving categories, understanding discount impact, and tracking high-demand products to support data-driven business decisions.

---

# Business Problem

The business lacked clear visibility into:

- Which product categories contribute the most revenue  
- How discounts influence customer purchasing behavior  
- Which products experience the highest demand  
- Inventory and stock availability patterns  
- Product pricing trends across categories  

The goal was to identify key business drivers, optimize inventory planning, and improve overall sales performance.

---

# Dataset Description

The dataset contains Zepto quick-commerce sales and product data, including:

- Product details (Product Name, Category)  
- Pricing information (MRP, Discounted Price, Discount %)  
- Product availability and stock status  
- Sales-related metrics and inventory fields  
- Category and product-level attributes  

The raw dataset required cleaning and transformation before analysis and dashboard development.

---

# Data Cleaning & Preparation

## Using SQL

- Removed duplicate and inconsistent records  
- Standardized product categories and pricing fields  
- Handled null and missing values  
- Created calculated fields for discount and pricing analysis  
- Structured cleaned output for dashboard integration  

## Dashboard Preparation

- Exported the cleaned SQL output into a structured dataset  
- Connected processed data to Power BI for visualization and KPI tracking

# Key Insights (With Metrics)

- A small group of product categories contributes to the majority of overall sales (~40–50%), indicating strong category concentration  

- Products with higher discount percentages show increased purchase activity, highlighting the influence of pricing strategies on customer behavior  

- Frequently purchased low-priced products generate high transaction volume, reflecting quick-commerce convenience buying patterns  

- Certain categories consistently outperform others in terms of sales contribution and product demand  

- Inventory analysis revealed that a subset of products experiences frequent stock pressure due to high customer demand  

---

# Business Recommendations

- Prioritize inventory management for high-demand products to reduce stock shortages  

- Optimize discount strategies to balance customer acquisition and profitability  

- Increase visibility and promotions for top-performing product categories  

- Improve stock forecasting during high-demand periods  

- Focus marketing efforts on products with consistently strong sales performance
